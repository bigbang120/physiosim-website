// Step 4 — Wavelet deviation detection

Frequency-domain analysis — what the wavelet found

PhysioSim applies Discrete Wavelet Transform (db4 Daubechies) to detect when signal rhythms shift — not just whether they did. This is more clinically precise than FFT for non-stationary physiological waveforms.

Signal Baseline Freq (Hz) Recent Freq (Hz) Freq Shift Energy Ratio Deviated? Clinical Significance
ECG 0.0004 0.0134 +0.013 Hz 0.111 YES Meaningful shift in cardiac rhythm frequency
Plethysmogram 0.000 0.000 0.000 0.076 YES Energy reduction in PPG waveform
Temperature 0.000 0.000 0.000 0.087 YES Spectral energy shift vs baseline window
Accelerometer 0.0047 0.0048 0.0001 0.092 YES Minimal frequency shift, energy change detected

Note: Energy ratios below 1.0 indicate reduced spectral energy in the recent window vs baseline — consistent with a period of reduced physiological activity (e.g., sedation, sleep, reduced movement). PhysioSim flags both increases and decreases as warranting clinical review.

⚡

Zero hardware overhead

Input: a CSV file. Output: structured JSON + PDF report. No device required. No proprietary format. Any data source that can export CSV works with PhysioSim.

Important context: This proof of concept used high-frequency waveform data (125Hz ECG/PPG) — a technically demanding input type. For a clinical chronic care pilot, we would use daily RPM readings (heart rate, weight, SpO₂, blood pressure) — a simpler, more clinically interpretable data format where our individualized baseline approach is most actionable. This demonstration shows the engine can handle waveform data as well.
